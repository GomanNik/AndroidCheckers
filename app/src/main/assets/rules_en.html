Checkers Rules

A short overview of classic rules. The game uses an 8×8 board and diagonal movement.

Goal of the Game

Win the game by capturing all opponent’s pieces or by leaving them with no legal moves.

Starting Position

  • The game is played on an 8×8 board using dark squares.
  • Each player has 12 pieces.
  • Pieces are placed on the dark squares of the first three rows on each side.
  • The player with the white pieces always makes the first move.

Movement of a Man

  • A man moves one square forward diagonally.
  • Moving onto an occupied square is not allowed.
  • A man cannot move backward until it becomes a king.

The King

  • A man reaching the opponent’s last row becomes a king.
  • A king moves diagonally forward or backward any number of free squares.
  • A king captures by jumping over an opponent’s piece diagonally as well.

Capturing

General capture rules:

  • A capture is made by jumping over an opponent’s piece onto the free square behind it.
  • The jumped piece is removed from the board.
  • If another capture is possible with the same piece, the player must continue the capturing sequence.

Mandatory capture rule:

  • If a capture is available, a player must perform it.
  • If multiple capture sequences exist, the player may choose any of them.

End of the Game

  • Win — the opponent has no pieces left or has no legal moves.
  • Loss — your pieces are gone or you cannot move.
  • Draw may occur:
    • by mutual agreement;
    • if no captures or progress happen for a long time;
    • if the same position repeats several times.

App Features

Modes and Settings

  • Mandatory capture — if enabled, you must capture whenever possible.
  • Move hints — when selecting a piece, all legal moves are highlighted.
  • Background theme — switch between light and dark themes for the board and menus.
  • Play as — choose to play as white or black.
  • Language — switch between interface languages (for example, Russian / English).

Difficulty Levels

Level Description
Easy Simple AI moves, good for beginners.
Medium More careful play, AI considers basic combinations.
Hard AI looks deeper and may use sacrifices.
Expert Requires precise play; mistakes are punished quickly.
Grandmaster Maximum difficulty for experienced players.

Note: actual AI behavior depends on the implemented algorithm and may differ from the classical descriptions.

These rules serve as a quick reminder. In disputable cases, follow the official Russian Checkers rules or agreements made before the game.
